# Reflection

## How do I approach designing software?

I approach designing software by first understanding the overall objective and then breaking it into smaller, manageable components. In this project, I focused on how real-world objects could be represented using simple geometric primitives, ensuring that each component had a clear role in the overall scene.

I prioritized structure and clarity before complexity, which helped maintain control over the development process and reduced errors.


## What new design skills has your work on the project helped you to craft?

This project strengthened my ability to apply **spatial reasoning and visual decomposition**. I learned how to take real-world objects and represent them using basic shapes while maintaining recognizable structure.

I also developed a better understanding of **scene composition**, including object placement, scaling, and how lighting interacts with surfaces to create depth.


## What design process did you follow for your project work?

I followed an incremental design process:

1. Establish the base scene
2. Add objects one at a time
3. Apply transformations and positioning
4. Introduce textures
5. Implement lighting
6. Refine and adjust

This step-by-step approach made debugging easier and ensured that each part of the scene functioned correctly before moving forward.


## How could tactics from your design approach be applied in future work?

The structured and incremental approach used in this project can be applied to any software development task. Breaking problems into smaller components and building progressively helps maintain clarity and reduces the risk of major issues.

This approach is especially valuable in large systems where multiple components must interact correctly.


## How do I approach developing programs?

I approach development by implementing core functionality first and then refining details through testing and iteration. I rely heavily on continuous testing rather than waiting until the end of development.

This allows me to identify and fix issues early, improving stability and reducing complexity later in the process.


## What new development strategies did you use while working on your 3D scene?

One key strategy I used was **iterative testing and refinement**. After each change, I immediately tested the scene to observe the impact.

I also learned to isolate variables when debugging, especially when working with lighting and transformations. Adjusting one parameter at a time made it easier to identify the cause of issues.


## How did iteration factor into your development?

Iteration played a major role in completing the project. The final scene was achieved through continuous adjustments rather than a single implementation.

Examples include:

* Refining lighting multiple times to achieve balance
* Adjusting object positions to ensure proper grounding
* Modifying colors and materials for better visibility

Each iteration improved the overall quality of the scene.


## How has your approach to developing code evolved throughout the milestones?

At the beginning, my focus was mainly on getting the code to work. Over time, my approach became more structured and intentional.

I began to:

* Plan before coding
* Test more frequently
* Debug more systematically
* Focus on organization and readability

By the final project, I had a clearer and more disciplined development process.
